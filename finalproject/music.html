  • Crush (크러쉬) is a South Korean singer-songwriter and producer under P Nation. He is a member of the project hip hop group FANXY CHILD and former one half of the duo Masterpiece. He officially debuted as a soloist on April 1, 2014 with the single "Sometimes". Prior to his debut, he released two other singles, "Red Dress" and "Crush On You".


  • DEAN (딘; sometimes styled as DΞΔN) is a South Korean alternative R&B singer-songwriter and record producer. He made his solo debut on March 24, 2016 with the mini album 130 Mood: TRBL. He is a member of the project hip hop group FANXY CHILD.


  • Lee Hi (이하이) born as Lee Ha-yi on Sept 28, 1996 is a South Korean soloist under YG Entertainment. She was born in Bucheon, South Korea. Lee Hi was best known as a runner up on Kpop Star 1. She debuted with the single 1, 2, 3, 4 on Oct 8, 2012 with her music video. Lee Hi is also a part of a duo Hi Suhyun consisting of Lee Suhyun and Lee Hi.

  • Pentatonix is a five-person American group of singers popular for acapella music. They are distinguished by their contemporary format and display unique strengths at each position. They have enjoyed the top place with ten albums, many Grammy awards and international popularity this century. Officially formed in 2011, the group proceeded to with the NBC TV acapella completion. Since then, the five have recorded multiple song videos before trying out their original compositions.


  • Ariana Grande began performing onstage when she was a child. Her involvement in a Broadway play at age 15, followed by some small TV parts, helped her land the role of Cat on TV's Victorious. She followed that with the spinoff Sam & Cat and then dove headfirst into a chart-topping musical career, releasing five albums: Yours Truly (2013), My Everything (2014), Dangerous Woman (2016), Sweetener (2018) and Thank U, Next (2019).


  • Elizabeth Woolridge Grant (born June 21, 1985), known by her stage name Lana Del Rey, is an American singer-songwriter. Her music is noted for its stylized, cinematic quality; themes of sadness, tragic romance, glamor, and melancholia; and references to pop culture, particularly 1950s and 1960s Americana. Born in New York City and raised in upstate New York, Del Rey returned to New York City in 2005 to begin her music career. Following numerous projects, including her self-titled debut studio album, Del Rey's breakthrough came with the viral success of her debut single "Video Games" in 2011. She signed with Interscope and Polydor later that year. Her major label debut, Born to Die (2012), was an international success and spawned a top-ten single "Summertime Sadness" on the Billboard Hot 100, as well as the singles "Blue Jeans" and "Born to Die", which charted in several overseas territories. She also attended Fordham University for College.
